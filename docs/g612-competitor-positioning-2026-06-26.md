Accepted as a research/strategy packet. This is not a legal claim or pricing decision.

## Product Thesis

Heavy Chain should not compete as a generic image generator. The 10M-user path is to become the apparel-specific production OS that connects:

1. Material upload and recognition.
2. AI fitting, model planning, print/design, campaign, video, and lab workflows.
3. Canvas/Gallery/History/Jobs continuity.
4. Real generation quality gates and recovery from worker/model failure.
5. Reusable brand/project memory for repeated apparel production.

## Priority Implications

1. Keep Lightchain-like information architecture as the default shell: `おすすめ`, `企画デザインツール`, `AIフィッティング`, `グラフィックツール`.
2. Make every major feature start from visual material upload or selectable presets, not long text-first forms.
3. Treat Canvas/Gallery/Jobs as product differentiators, not side features.
4. Add eventual Shopify/export integrations only after final G615 proof, because ecommerce distribution matters but should not disturb the core UX now.
5. Avoid broad Canva/Adobe-style general-purpose expansion until apparel workflows feel inevitable.
